About The Economist

The Analyst Behind the Advisory

The Food Economist Advisory exists because most food businesses — regardless of size — operate without the economic intelligence they need to make consistently good decisions. They rely on intuition, year-end figures, and advice from generalists who have never managed a food cost or read a menu matrix.

Godfred Frimpong founded this practice after three decades working at the intersection of food sector economics and financial management — advising businesses ranging from independent operators to food manufacturers serving major UK retailers.

"My background is unusual. I combine the analytical discipline of professional financial qualifications with an MSc dedicated specifically to food economics. There are very few practitioners who occupy both spaces — and that combination is precisely why this advisory works."

The food and hospitality sector is unlike any other. Margins are thin, costs move daily, consumer behaviour is unpredictable, and the regulatory environment — from EPR packaging obligations to food safety compliance — is tightening. Generic business advice does not translate. Economic thinking specific to food does.

Every engagement is conducted personally. There are no junior associates, no templated reports, and no recycled recommendations. What you receive is the result of applying three decades of food sector economic experience to your specific situation — supported by two proprietary technology platforms that provide the data integrity most consultants never achieve.

The Food Business Intelligence System™

All advisory work is underpinned by a proprietary methodology that combines food economics modelling, live cost intelligence (via Flivio), and operational data capture (via Restaurant-IQ) to produce analysis of a standard typically reserved for institutional investors — applied to real food businesses of every size.
