## PROFESSIONAL BODY RISK MANAGEMENT (FCCA)

**The constraint:** As an FCCA, Godfred cannot hold himself out as offering audit, statutory accounts preparation, or other reserved public practice services without an ACCA practising certificate.

**The solution — always frame services as:**
- Food Business Economics (not "accounting")
- Management Information (not "management accounts preparation")
- Economic Modelling (not "bookkeeping")
- Food Finance Advisory (not "financial advice")
- Business Performance Analysis (not "audit")
- Operational Economics (not "cost accounting")

**Safe positioning line:** "Our advisory combines professional financial qualifications with deep food sector economics expertise — delivering institutional-grade analysis within the food and hospitality industry."

The FCCA credential is displayed as a quality mark, not as an offer of accountancy services.

Position it like a boutique specialist consultancy with two clearly defined practice divisions. Not a personal website. Not a freelancer. A specialist advisory group.

```
thefoodeconomist.co.uk          ← Group homepage (audience hub)
    │
    ├── thefoodeconomist.co.uk/advisory      ← Restaurant & Foodservice Advisory
    │       (OR: advisory.thefoodeconomist.co.uk)
    │
    ├── thefoodeconomist.co.uk/compliance    ← EPR & Packaging Compliance (EXISTING)
    │       (restructure current homepage into this section)
    │
    ├── restaurantiq.thefoodeconomist.co.uk  ← Restaurant-IQ App landing page
    │
    └── flivio.thefoodeconomist.co.uk        ← Flivio App landing page
```

**Why this structure (not separate domains):**
- All authority, backlinks, and trust stays under one domain — SEO benefit
- Looks like a group operation, not a freelancer with side projects
- Subdomains indexed separately by Google — clean audience segmentation
- Easy navigation between divisions (shared nav component)
- One brand, multiple specialist divisions — like McKinsey Food Practice vs McKinsey Digital

## CONTENT STRATEGY — HOW TO AVOID "JACK OF ALL TRADES"

**Problem:** 10 services across accounting, operations, tech, and strategy — looks scattered.

**Solution: The 4-Practice-Area Framework**

Never list 10 services. Always present 4 practice areas. Each area has 2–3 services underneath. This is how Big 4, law firms, and boutique consultancies present complexity.

**Framing:**
> "The Food Economist Advisory operates across four specialist practice areas, each informed by the same core discipline: food economics applied to real business decisions."

This makes it a *firm*, not a person with many services.

**The proprietary methodology branding (critical for authority):**

Each practice area should have a named methodology:

- Strategic Economics → "The Food Business Valuation Framework™"
- Operational Economics → "The Margin Architecture Programme™"
- Financial Economics → "The Food Business Intelligence System™" (powered by Restaurant-IQ + Flivio)
- Retained Advisory → "The Embedded Food Finance Director™ Model"

These invented methodology names do exactly what "The Statutory Liability Engine™" does on the existing site — they make it sound proprietary, systematic, and expert.

## CONVERSION OPTIMISATION STRATEGY

**Primary conversion goal:** Book a discovery call / submit an enquiry

**Secondary:** Download a resources (lead capture)

**Conversion levers:**

1. **Urgency without panic** — Not "your restaurant is failing" but "the operators who know their economics outperform those who don't"
2. **Specificity** — Use real numbers (food cost targets, EBITDA margins) to demonstrate expertise
3. **Social proof** — Credential bar visible on every page, above the fold
4. **Multiple CTAs** — Book a call, WhatsApp, email — meet the prospect where they are
5. **Risk reversal** — Free 30-minute discovery call (removes the price barrier for first contact)
6. **Anchoring** — Show the most expensive service first (sale & valuation at £8,000+) so mid-range feels reasonable
7. **Reciprocity** — Insights Hub with real, valuable content — earns trust before the sale
