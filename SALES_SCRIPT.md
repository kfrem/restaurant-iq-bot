# Restaurant-IQ Bot — Sales Script for Field Agents

---

## QUICK PITCH (30 seconds)

"Restaurant-IQ Bot turns your team's everyday Telegram messages — voice notes, invoice photos, texts — into a full weekly intelligence report. No extra apps, no training, no data entry. Your staff just talks. You get the insights."

---

## WHO TO SELL TO

**Primary targets:**
- Independent restaurant owners (1–5 locations)
- Cafe and bar owners
- Food truck operators
- Dark kitchen / ghost kitchen operators

**Best contact:** The owner or general manager — the person who currently reviews costs, waste, and staff reports manually.

---

## THE PROBLEM (Open with this)

Start by asking ONE of these questions:

> "How do you currently track what's happening in your kitchen day to day?"

> "If I asked you right now what your biggest cost driver was last week, how long would it take to find out?"

> "How many hours a week do you or your managers spend pulling together reports?"

Let them talk. Common pain points you'll hear:

- "I rely on my managers telling me things verbally"
- "I look at the numbers at end of month and by then it's too late"
- "My staff don't fill in logs properly"
- "I'm never on-site to see what's actually happening"

---

## THE SOLUTION (After they've shared their pain)

"That's exactly what Restaurant-IQ was built for. Here's how it works:

**Step 1 — Your team already uses Telegram.** You create one group for the restaurant. Staff just send messages like they normally would — a voice note at the end of a shift, a photo of an invoice when it arrives, a quick text about a customer complaint or a busy lunch rush.

**Step 2 — The bot captures everything automatically.** Voice notes get transcribed. Invoice photos are read for supplier name, total, and line items. Text updates are tagged by category — waste, revenue, staffing, issues.

**Step 3 — Every week, you get a full report.** One command — `/weeklyreport` — and within minutes you have:
- A structured briefing sent directly in Telegram
- A branded PDF attached — ready to print or share with your accountant"

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

**"My staff won't use it."**
> "They don't need to learn anything new. If they can send a WhatsApp voice note, they can use this. It's just a Telegram group — which most of them already have. No app, no login, no training."

**"We already use [system X] for our POS / inventory."**
> "This doesn't replace your POS. It captures the human intelligence that your POS misses — why you had a bad night, which supplier keeps sending short deliveries, what the kitchen team flagged at 11pm. Think of it as the layer between your staff's brains and your reports."

**"Is my data safe?"**
> "Everything runs locally on your machine. No data goes to any cloud, no third party sees your invoices or your business figures. It's the opposite of most SaaS tools."

**"What does it cost?"**
> [Insert your pricing here. If usage-based or one-time setup, explain that here.]

**"What if it breaks?"**
> "It's designed to be simple and robust. But if anything goes wrong, [insert support contact / SLA here]."

**"We're too small for this."**
> "That's actually who it's built for. Big chains have whole operations teams. You're the one doing everything yourself — this is your operations team in a bot."

## THE CLOSE

Once they're interested, guide them here:

> "The setup takes about 15 minutes. You need a Telegram group for your team and we handle the rest. Want me to show you what a sample weekly report looks like?"

**If you have a sample PDF:** Show it. The PDF is the most convincing asset.

**Trial close:**
> "Would it make sense to run it for one week — no cost, no commitment — and see what comes back in the first report?"

**Hard close:**
> "I can get you set up today. What's the name of your restaurant?"

2. Show the bot processing it (or a screenshot of the transcript + analysis)
3. Show the `/weeklyreport` command output in Telegram

**Key line during demo:**
> "Your chef sends this at the end of service. You wake up Friday morning and you already know exactly what happened all week."
